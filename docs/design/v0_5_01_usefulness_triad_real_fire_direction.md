# AWSRT v0.5 Opening Design Note: Usefulness-Triad Real-Fire Direction

**Status:** Draft opening design note  
**Applies to:** `v0.5-subgoal-01`  
**Purpose:** Define the opening direction of AWSRT v0.5 by shifting from bounded active-family validation toward the compact usefulness-controller line, asking whether the usefulness triad can now be made scientifically readable and operationally inspectable on transformed real-fire conditions without collapsing it into the broader regime-managed control layer.

---

## 1. Purpose of this note

This note defines the opening direction of AWSRT v0.5.

AWSRT v0.4 should now be read as having completed an important bounded controller-facing checkpoint. Across a compact cross-window transformed real-fire bundle, the currently accepted active-family surface remained readable enough that immediate family redesign was no longer justified. In particular:

* corruption-sensitive remained the most stable family identity,
* opportunistic remained clearly distinguishable across contexts,
* balanced remained usable but still somewhat close to corruption-sensitive,
* and the repeated weak boundary was identified honestly rather than hidden.

That result matters because it changes what the next version should do.

The next question is no longer:

> Do the currently accepted active families survive a first bounded cross-window real-fire validation pass?

The next question is now:

> Can AWSRT move forward on the compact usefulness-controller line itself, and can the usefulness triad become scientifically readable on transformed real-fire conditions without being conflated with the broader regime-managed layer?

This is the right opening question for v0.5 because the broader AWSRT scientific thread still contains an unfinished line. At AWSRT v0.1, one of the most important findings was that **information delivered** and the **operational usefulness** of that information could diverge. Since then, AWSRT advanced substantially on controller-facing operational layers, especially through regime-managed and active-family work, but the compact usefulness-controller line has not yet become the main real-fire-facing subject of a disciplined development phase.

AWSRT v0.5 should therefore open by returning to that thread directly.

---

## 2. Background and framing

### 2.1 What earlier phases established

The relevant AWSRT progression should now be read as follows.

* **AWSRT v0.1** established the core scientific wedge: information delivery and belief-useful information are not the same thing, especially under impairment.
* **AWSRT v0.2** clarified several controller-facing and usefulness-adjacent surfaces so that interpretation became cleaner and more truthful.
* **AWSRT v0.3** made bounded transformed real-fire experimentation operationally tractable.
* **AWSRT v0.4** pushed the controller-facing real-fire side further by stabilizing, comparing, and cross-validating active-family behavior across bounded transformed real-fire contexts.

This means AWSRT has now done substantial work on the **regime layer**, but has not yet fully turned the **usefulness layer** into the next disciplined real-fire-facing line of study.

### 2.2 The two main operational-control layers

At the current AWSRT stage, the operational-control story has two different layers.

#### A. Usefulness layer

This is the compact usefulness-controller surface organized around the triad:

* exploit
* recover
* caution

This layer is best understood as the compact information-health or usefulness-facing control layer.

Its role is narrower than the broader regime-managed layer. It is not primarily trying to express the full deployment-control structure. It is trying to express the condition of the information stream in a way that can drive a compact and readable control response.

#### B. Regime layer

This is the broader regime-managed control surface.

It includes:

* advisory regime semantics,
* active realized regime behavior,
* regime-family structure,
* and the nominal / downshift / certified active-state reading.

This layer is best understood as the higher-level deployment-control layer.

### 2.3 Why v0.5 should begin with the usefulness layer

The regime layer is not finished forever, but it is now bounded enough that it does not appear to require immediate reopening as the first task of the next version.

The usefulness layer, by contrast, still represents a scientifically central but less fully developed line. It is also the layer most directly tied to the earlier wedge question:

* not just whether information is arriving,
* but whether it is still operationally useful,
* whether it is stale,
* whether it is misleading,
* and whether control should exploit, recover, or become cautious.

That makes the usefulness triad the right opening direction for AWSRT v0.5.

## 3. Problem statement

The current problem can be stated as follows:

> After bounded cross-window validation of the active-family surface in AWSRT v0.4, it remains unclear whether the compact usefulness-controller triad can now be made scientifically readable and operationally inspectable on transformed real-fire conditions as its own real development line, rather than remaining only a partially embedded or secondary control surface.

The key questions are:

* Can the exploit / recover / caution triad be made legible on transformed real-fire runs?
* Can it be evaluated without collapsing it into the regime-family layer?
* Are its triggers and state occupancies scientifically interpretable across bounded real-fire contexts?
* Does it recover the earlier usefulness-wedge intuition in a more operationally explicit way?
* Is the triad already strong enough to deserve a dedicated v0.5 development line, or does it still need prior cleanup before that becomes meaningful?

This is therefore not primarily a family-comparison problem anymore. It is a usefulness-layer legibility problem.

---

## 4. Subgoal decision

AWSRT v0.5 should begin with a **bounded usefulness-triad real-fire direction subgoal**.

It should be:

* **not** a broad regime-family redesign,
* **not** a broad new synthetic sweep campaign,
* **not** an attempt to merge the usefulness and regime layers prematurely,
* **not** yet a full thesis-results chapter expansion,

but **yes** to:

* making the usefulness triad more explicit as its own control layer,
* checking whether exploit / recover / caution state behavior is readable on transformed real-fire runs,
* tightening any obvious truthfulness or instrumentation gaps that prevent that reading,
* and deciding whether the usefulness-controller line is now strong enough to become the main next-phase scientific thread.

This keeps the opening v0.5 scope disciplined while turning directly toward one of the most important unresolved AWSRT lines.

### 4.1 Immediate Subgoal 01 reading

The immediate task of `v0.5-subgoal-01` should be read narrowly and in disciplined terms.

It is **not** to redesign the controller stack.  
It is **not** to reopen active-family comparison as the main line.  
It is **not** to unify the usefulness layer with the regime layer.

It **is** to establish whether the compact usefulness triad can be treated as a truthful, visible, and interpretable control layer on bounded transformed real-fire conditions.

In practice, this means the first subgoal should ask four bounded questions:

1. **Truthfulness**  
   Do exploit / recover / caution still correspond to defensible information-health conditions under transformed real-fire runs?

2. **Visibility**  
   Can AWSRT currently show enough about usefulness-state occupancy, trigger context, and recent-window driver conditions to make the triad inspectable?

3. **Tightness**  
   Are there obvious compressions, ambiguities, or misassignments in the triad reading that can be corrected with small, auditable refinements?

4. **Layer separation**  
   Can the usefulness layer be interpreted on its own terms rather than disappearing into broader advisory / active and nominal / downshift / certified semantics?

This is the first real claim `v0.5-subgoal-01` should try to support.

## 5. What v0.5 Subgoal 01 should focus on

### 5.1 Separate usefulness-layer interpretation from regime-layer interpretation

The first task is conceptual and structural.

The project should avoid treating the usefulness triad as merely a shadow of the regime layer.

The usefulness layer should instead be interpreted on its own terms:

* exploit as healthy-usefulness behavior,
* recover as delayed, stale, or degraded-but-restorable usefulness behavior,
* caution as corruption-like or seriously compromised usefulness behavior.

This separation matters scientifically. Without it, the usefulness line risks disappearing into the larger regime-managed story.

### 5.2 Inspect current usefulness instrumentation on real-fire runs

The second task is diagnostic.

The current operational real-fire path should be inspected to determine whether the usefulness-controller outputs are already sufficiently visible and truthful to support disciplined interpretation.

Questions here include:

* Are exploit / recover / caution occupancies emitted cleanly?
* Are trigger-hit counts available and trustworthy?
* Are the recent-window usefulness signals still semantically aligned with the intended triad reading?
* Can bounded transformed real-fire runs already expose a readable usefulness profile without major code motion?

### 5.3 Prefer bounded tightening over controller expansion

The opening v0.5 move should not be to add many new usefulness states, thresholds, or policy branches.

Instead, it should prefer:

* bounded semantics tightening,
* bounded instrumentation improvement,
* bounded visualization or summary clarification,
* and bounded real-fire verification.

The goal is to determine whether the compact triad is already scientifically viable, not to make it complicated.

### 5.4 Use bounded real-fire slices, not a full campaign

As in late v0.4, the first v0.5 step should stay compact.

The usefulness-triad line should first be examined on a small curated transformed real-fire bundle rather than through a large new campaign.

The question is:

* does the triad read as a real and interpretable control layer under bounded real-fire conditions?

not yet:

* can AWSRT prove full usefulness-controller generality across the whole transformed real-fire design space?

### 5.5 End with a clear decision about the usefulness line

The first v0.5 subgoal should end with an explicit decision.

It should decide whether:

1. the usefulness triad is now strong enough to become the main next-phase development line,
2. the usefulness layer still needs one more bounded truthfulness / instrumentation pass,
3. or the triad remains too under-specified and should not yet be treated as the main controller-facing scientific thread.

## 6. Proposed v0.5 opening direction

### 6.1 Core recommendation

The recommended opening direction is:

* make the compact usefulness-controller layer the main bounded subject of v0.5,
* verify its exploit / recover / caution reading on transformed real-fire conditions,
* and keep it explicitly distinct from the broader regime-managed layer.

The intended shift is therefore:

* from “the regime-family surface survives bounded real-fire variation,”
* to “the usefulness-controller triad itself can now be examined as a scientifically meaningful real-fire control layer.”

### 6.2 Backend direction

Backend work should remain small, explicit, and usefulness-facing.

Likely acceptable directions include:

#### A. Inspect usefulness-controller emitted fields

Check whether current summaries and run outputs expose the usefulness-layer signals needed for disciplined interpretation.

#### B. Tighten only what blocks truthful usefulness reading

If exploit / recover / caution outputs are present but ambiguous, patch only what is required to make them readable and trustworthy.

#### C. Avoid premature unification with regime logic

Do not turn this opening subgoal into a controller-unification effort unless the evidence clearly requires it.

### 6.3 Frontend / analysis direction

Frontend or analysis work, if needed, should remain bounded and in service of usefulness-layer inspection.

Acceptable changes would include:

* small updates so usefulness-triad summaries are visible,
* wording fixes so exploit / recover / caution semantics are not confused with nominal / downshift / certified,
* compact visual or summary surfaces for bounded real-fire usefulness reading.

This is not yet a broad UI redesign.

### 6.4 Likely first code touchpoints

The most likely first files to inspect are:

#### A. `backend/api/routers/operational.py`

This is the most likely first backend touchpoint because it has historically held the live usefulness-controller path, emitted controller summaries, active/advisory reporting surfaces, and compact diagnostic logic.

It matters because `v0.5-subgoal-01` first needs to answer:

* where exploit / recover / caution logic currently lives,
* what recent-window or driver signals currently feed it,
* what usefulness-facing fields are already emitted,
* and where usefulness-layer reporting may still be entangled with regime-layer reporting.

#### B. `backend/awsrt_core/schemas/operational.py`

This is the next likely backend touchpoint because it defines the declared configuration surface and therefore sets the truthfulness boundary for what the system claims to expose.

It matters because the first v0.5 step should verify:

* whether the usefulness-controller surface is declared honestly,
* whether old surface mismatches remain,
* and whether any new field is truly necessary before adding it.

The default preference should be to avoid schema expansion unless inspection shows a real truthfulness gap.

#### C. `frontend/app/operational/designer/page.tsx`

This is the first likely frontend inspection target.

It matters because the designer controls how the usefulness layer is exposed, named, and conceptually separated from the regime layer. The first question is whether the current surface already lets a user treat exploit / recover / caution as a compact usefulness-facing layer rather than as a confusing shadow of broader regime semantics.

#### D. `frontend/app/operational/visualizer/page.tsx`

This is the next likely frontend touchpoint.

It matters because operational inspectability will likely depend on whether usefulness state, occupancy, trigger context, or compact audit summaries are visible enough to support bounded real-fire interpretation.

#### E. `frontend/app/analysis/batch/page.tsx`

This is a likely later touchpoint for the opening step, especially if a compact bounded verification slice needs a small preset or batch-facing convenience update.

It should come after backend truthfulness and frontend visibility are understood.

## 7. Success criteria

The opening v0.5 subgoal should be considered successful if it produces most or all of the following.

1. **The usefulness layer is treated as its own operational-control layer**  
   The exploit / recover / caution triad should no longer read as only an implicit subcomponent of the regime layer.

2. **Usefulness-triad behavior becomes inspectable on transformed real-fire runs**  
   The project should be able to see and interpret the compact usefulness-controller behavior on bounded real-fire conditions.

3. **Truthfulness gaps are tightened if needed**  
   Any obvious instrumentation or summary-layer ambiguity that blocks disciplined reading should be fixed.

4. **The usefulness wedge is reconnected to the current controller-facing platform state**  
   The project should regain a direct bridge between the v0.1 delivered-versus-useful-information line and the present real-fire-facing controller stack.

5. **The next-step decision becomes evidence-based**  
   At the end of the subgoal, the project should know whether the usefulness triad is ready to become the main v0.5 development line.

6. **Claims remain scientifically modest**  
   The work should be framed as bounded usefulness-layer verification and clarification, not as proof of a final usefulness controller.

## 8. Disciplined implementation plan

### 8.1 Step A: preserve the v0.4 closing state

Begin from the v0.4 closeout state without reopening active-family redesign.

### 8.2 Step B: inspect the current usefulness-controller surface

Read the current backend outputs, summaries, and any visible frontend surfaces with exploit / recover / caution interpretation in mind.

Identify:

* what is already readable,
* what is ambiguous,
* what is stale or misleading,
* and what would block bounded real-fire usefulness inspection.

### 8.3 Step C: implement only bounded truthfulness or visibility fixes

Make only the changes needed to support disciplined usefulness-layer reading.

Avoid broad expansion.

### 8.4 Step D: run one bounded real-fire usefulness verification slice

Use a compact transformed real-fire bundle to determine whether the usefulness triad expresses a meaningful and interpretable control pattern.

### 8.5 Step E: interpret the usefulness line honestly

At the end of the bounded slice, ask:

* does the triad read clearly?
* do exploit / recover / caution meanings survive contact with transformed real-fire conditions?
* is the usefulness layer now ready to become the main next-phase scientific thread?

### 8.6 Step F: freeze with a next-step decision

Document:

* what the usefulness layer currently expresses,
* what remains weak or ambiguous,
* and whether AWSRT v0.5 should continue directly into a larger usefulness-triad phase.

### 8.7 Recommended first implementation sequence

The first implementation sequence should stay as small as possible.

#### A. Inspect the live usefulness-controller path first

Begin with `backend/api/routers/operational.py`.

The first inspection should answer:

* where the live exploit / recover / caution logic currently sits,
* what signals and recent-window summaries currently drive it,
* what run-summary fields and audits are already emitted,
* and where usefulness-layer reporting still appears mixed with regime-layer semantics.

This is the key first backend reading step.

#### B. Check declared-surface honesty next

Then inspect `backend/awsrt_core/schemas/operational.py`.

The purpose here is to verify:

* whether the declared usefulness-controller surface matches the live behavior,
* whether any field implies more than the code actually supports,
* and whether any schema change is truly needed before implementation begins.

#### C. Inspect designer and visualizer for usefulness-layer readability

Then inspect:

* `frontend/app/operational/designer/page.tsx`
* `frontend/app/operational/visualizer/page.tsx`

The first frontend questions are:

* is the usefulness layer clearly separated from the regime layer?
* can a user see enough to interpret exploit / recover / caution behavior?
* is wording scientifically modest and semantically honest?
* are there small visibility fixes that would help more than controller changes?

#### D. Apply the smallest credible patch

Only after inspection should code changes begin.

The preferred first patch type is:

* usefulness-layer reporting cleanup,
* usefulness-state visibility improvement,
* or wording/summary clarification.

Behavioral controller changes should come only if a first bounded slice exposes a clear truthfulness problem.

#### E. Run one bounded transformed real-fire verification slice

After the smallest patch, run one compact slice designed to test whether all three usefulness states are readable and interpretable.

This should be used to decide whether further tightening is needed.

## 10. Recommended first bounded real-fire verification slice

The first verification slice should stay deliberately small and manually interpretable.

A good opening slice is:

* one bounded transformed real-fire window, or at most two for a minimal robustness check,
* with three compact impairment conditions:
  * a healthy or low-impairment condition,
  * a delay-heavy condition,
  * and a noise-heavy condition.

The rationale is straightforward:

* the healthy condition should test whether exploit remains the dominant usefulness reading under good information-health conditions,
* the delay-heavy condition should test whether recover still expresses the stale-but-restorable side of usefulness degradation,
* the noise-heavy condition should test whether caution remains associated with corruption-like or seriously compromised usefulness rather than merely reduced arrival.

This is the cleanest first scientific slice because it directly probes whether the earlier delivered-information versus usefulness distinction remains visible in compact usefulness-state behavior.

The opening question is not whether the triad is universally robust across all transformed real-fire cases.

The opening question is whether the triad still produces a truthful and interpretable compact reading on a bounded but meaningful real-fire slice.

---

## 11. Scope boundary

This opening v0.5 subgoal is intentionally limited.

It is **not**:

* a full controller unification effort,
* a broad regime-family redesign,
* a full real-fire usefulness campaign,
* a final usefulness-results chapter,
* or proof that the compact usefulness triad is already the final AWSRT controller.

It **is**:

* a bounded opening step for AWSRT v0.5,
* a direct return to the usefulness thread,
* a test of whether exploit / recover / caution can now become a scientifically readable real-fire control layer,
* and a disciplined way to decide whether the usefulness-triad line should lead the next phase.

That is the most appropriate opening direction for AWSRT v0.5 after the bounded family-validation closeout of v0.4.
