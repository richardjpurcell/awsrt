# AWSRT v0.4 Subgoal 03: Corruption-Family Active Expression Without Opportunistic Collapse

**Status:** Draft design note  
**Applies to:** `v0.4-subgoal-03`  
**Purpose:** Define the next bounded controller-facing step after the Subgoal 02 freeze by making the corruption-focused active family behaviorally expressible on bounded real-fire windows through a bounded corruption-specific guarded path, without flattening it into an opportunistic-family imitation.

---

## 1. Purpose of this note

This note defines the third disciplined subgoal of AWSRT v0.4.

Subgoal 01 established that bounded transformed real-fire windows are a valid controller-facing evaluation surface and that visibly non-nominal active behavior can be recovered there. Subgoal 02 then revisited family structure and showed that structure matters, but also clarified an important asymmetry:

- the **opportunistic** family remains the clearest proof that readable active movement is possible,
- the **balanced** family can be made somewhat more behaviorally legible through bounded structural revision,
- but the **corruption-focused** family still resists readable realized expression.

That changes the next question again.

The immediate problem is no longer mainly whether balanced and corruption families both need a general structure revisit. The immediate problem is whether the **corruption-focused family** can be given its own genuinely readable active expression **as a corruption-led family**, rather than as a weak copy of opportunistic behavior.

Subgoal 03 therefore asks:

> What is the smallest bounded corruption-family revision that can produce readable realized non-nominal active behavior on bounded real-fire windows while preserving a corruption-sensitive interpretation and avoiding opportunistic-family collapse?

Subgoal 03 may therefore include not only a bounded corruption-trigger refinement, but also one small corruption-specific structural addition if trigger refinement alone still fails to produce readable realized behavior.

The stance remains disciplined. This is not a whole-controller redesign. It is a bounded, family-specific clarification step motivated by the evidence produced in Subgoals 01 and 02.

---

## 2. Background and framing

### 2.1 v0.1 scientific story

AWSRT v0.1 established the scientific background that still governs controller-facing interpretation:

- time-to-first-detection is necessary but insufficient,
- informational activity and belief-improving value can diverge,
- and different forms of degradation matter differently.

This remains important here because the corruption family should not be read as “the family that moves whenever anything looks bad.” Its reason for existing is narrower and more specific: it is meant to express situations where information flow remains active enough to matter, yet becomes operationally less trustworthy or less belief-improving in a corruption-like way.

The corruption family therefore has a stronger semantic burden than a generic downshift family. It must not only move; it must move for reasons that can be interpreted as corruption-sensitive rather than merely support-thin or generally weak.

### 2.2 v0.2 interpretive cleanup

AWSRT v0.2 clarified the semantic boundaries that remain binding:

- usefulness remains lower-level evidence interpretation,
- regime remains higher-level posture,
- advisory remains recommendation-side,
- and active remains realized behavior.

This remains especially important for Subgoal 03 because corruption sensitivity is easy to overstate. A corruption-oriented family must not become a loose label for any difficult condition. If the family is to remain scientifically meaningful, its realized active behavior must still be explainable in terms of corruption-like degradation patterns rather than generic non-nominal pressure.

### 2.3 v0.3 bounded real-fire outcome

AWSRT v0.3 made bounded transformed real-fire windows scientifically usable enough for disciplined controller work and also showed that late corruption-side patching alone was not enough to restore the hoped-for active reading.

That result remains active here. It means the corruption-family problem is not hypothetical. It has already resisted one round of bounded trigger-side work.

### 2.4 v0.4 Subgoals 01 and 02 outcome

Subgoal 01 recovered readable active movement mainly through the opportunistic family and showed only marginal balanced improvement and little corruption improvement.

Subgoal 02 revisited family structure and pushed the balanced and corruption families somewhat farther. The key result, however, is that corruption still appears structurally and behaviorally underexpressed relative to its intended role.

This should be read carefully.

The corruption family is not failing only because no trigger ever fires. It appears to be failing because the current family form does not yet provide a sufficiently distinct, corruption-led path of realized posture that can become visible on bounded real-fire windows.

That is the specific motivation for Subgoal 03.

## 3. Control-language discipline for this subgoal

The control-language glossary remains active guidance.

For Subgoal 03:

- **usefulness** remains lower-level evidence about the operational value of the incoming information stream,
- **regime** remains a higher-level posture decision,
- **advisory** remains recommendation-side interpretation,
- **active** remains realized behavior,
- **corruption-focused family** means a bounded active-family configuration whose primary intended reading is corruption-like degradation, not generic support weakening.

This subgoal does **not** authorize making corruption simply another name for “more active than balanced.” It also does **not** authorize defining corruption by hidden latent truth or backend-only mismatch labels.

Instead, corruption-family behavior should remain readable through controller-visible quantities such as:

- misleadingness-like activity,
- persistent activity with weak realized information value,
- degraded information usefulness despite continuing arrivals,
- or other bounded observables that preserve a corruption-like interpretation.

The corruption family must remain semantically narrower than opportunistic.

## 4. Problem statement

The present problem can be stated more precisely as follows:

> On bounded transformed real-fire windows, the corruption-focused active family still does not express readable non-nominal realized behavior strongly enough to justify its intended family identity. We need a bounded revision that gives it a clearer corruption-led active path without collapsing it into opportunistic-family structure or generic weak-support behavior.

The issue is no longer merely that the corruption family lacks a threshold adjustment. The issue is whether it has a **family-specific realized path** that can become visible while still remaining honestly interpretable as corruption-sensitive.

This means Subgoal 03 should be judged less by raw transition count and more by whether corruption-family behavior becomes:

- visibly realized,
- causally corruption-led,
- semantically narrower than opportunistic,
- and experimentally readable on bounded real-fire windows.

---

## 5. Why the corruption family still appears underexpressed

The most plausible explanations are the following.

### 5.1 Corruption semantics may still be too dependent on rare conjunctions

The current corruption-facing path likely depends on a fairly specific conjunction of conditions:

- misleading activity elevated,
- arrivals still sufficiently active,
- and recent true information sufficiently low.

That conjunction is semantically clean, but may occur too rarely or too briefly on bounded real-fire windows to support readable realized posture.

If so, the corruption family can remain nominal not because corruption-like pressure is absent, but because the family only recognizes a very narrow extreme expression of it.

### 5.2 The family may lack a readable guarded corruption posture

A family often becomes readable not only because it has triggers, but because it has somewhere meaningful to go. Opportunistic already showed this. Balanced began to show it after some structural revision. Corruption may still lack a sufficiently distinct intermediate guarded posture that reads as corruption-led without immediately becoming certified-like or falling back nominally.

If the family has no meaningful guarded corruption rung, then even good corruption-facing signals may fail to produce a visible story.

### 5.3 Corruption-facing evidence may need accumulation rather than pointwise severity

Bounded real-fire windows may not produce long stretches of strongly corrupted-seeming signal at any single step. Instead, they may produce repeated smaller hints of degraded informational quality while arrivals remain active.

If the corruption family looks mostly for sharp pointwise evidence, it may miss the more realistic pattern: moderate but persistent corruption-like degradation.

### 5.4 Weak-support and corruption risk may still be entangled

Subgoal 01 and 02 showed that support weakening matters. But if the corruption family uses the same effective path as support-led families, then its realized behavior becomes hard to distinguish scientifically.

The problem, then, is not just getting the family to move. The problem is ensuring that when it moves, its movement can still be explained as corruption-led rather than support-led opportunistic behavior under another name.

### 5.5 Family identity may be too thinly encoded in active behavior

It is possible that the corruption family currently exists more in the preset description than in the realized state machine. If so, then the family label is ahead of the behavior.

Subgoal 03 should therefore treat this as a family-identity problem: the corruption family needs enough behavioral specificity that its active expression is recognizable as its own.

---

## 6. Subgoal decision: corruption-family clarification rather than broader family redesign

Subgoal 03 should **not** reopen all families again.

The recommended direction is:

- **not** a rework of opportunistic,
- **not** another broad family-structure sweep,
- **not** a merge of corruption and balanced,
- but **yes** to a bounded corruption-family clarification pass.

The reason is evidence-based. Subgoal 02 narrowed the remaining uncertainty. Balanced can now be treated as a family with at least some readable path. Opportunistic already serves as the reference success family. The remaining main controller-facing ambiguity is whether corruption can become behaviorally legible while preserving its intended meaning.

A targeted corruption-family clarification is therefore the smaller scientifically honest move.

---

## 7. Proposed v0.4 Subgoal 03 direction

### 7.1 Core recommendation

Subgoal 03 should introduce one bounded corruption-family active revision whose purpose is to make corruption-led realized posture visibly expressible on bounded real-fire windows without copying opportunistic-family structure wholesale.

The intended shift is:

- from a corruption family that is mostly nominal unless a narrow conjunction fires strongly,
- toward a corruption family that can express a guarded corruption-sensitive posture under persistent or repeated evidence of degraded informational usefulness.

### 7.2 Conceptual direction

The corruption family should likely become more willing to enter a **guarded corruption-sensitive downshift posture** when:

- arrivals remain active enough that the system is still “engaged,”
- misleadingness or corruption-like evidence is recurrent rather than necessarily extreme at one instant,
- and realized information contribution remains weak enough that nominal posture looks less justified.

This should remain different from opportunistic support collapse.

The desired reading is not:

- “support is weak, so downshift.”

The desired reading is closer to:

- “activity is still present, but the active stream is increasingly untrustworthy or weakly useful, so a guarded corruption-led posture becomes justified.”

### 7.3 Structural implication
The corruption family should be allowed one bounded additional active-path element, such as a corruption-sensitive guarded rung, a corruption-persistence accumulation path, or a corruption-led guarded downshift seam that becomes readable before certified intervention.

- a corruption-sensitive guarded rung,
- a corruption-specific persistence/accumulation path,
- or a corruption-sensitive downshift seam that is more structurally visible before certified intervention.

This does **not** require a large ladder. It requires only enough structure that the family can tell a readable story.

### 7.4 Distinction from opportunistic

The opportunistic family remains the reference case for readable active motion, but it should not become the direct template.

Subgoal 03 should preserve at least these distinctions:

- opportunistic responds mainly to weakening operational support or opportunity,
- corruption responds mainly to degraded usefulness/trustworthiness under continued activity,
- opportunistic may remain more mobility-shaped,
- corruption should remain more guarded and corruption-led.

### 7.5 What should not change

Subgoal 03 should not:

- collapse corruption into opportunistic,
- redefine corruption using inaccessible latent-state truth,
- redesign the whole regime-management system,
- explode the UI/schema into a large family-programming surface,
- or claim broad optimization of real-fire active control.

The change should remain compact, auditable, and family-specific.

---

## 8. Success criteria

Subgoal 03 should be considered successful if it produces all of the following on bounded transformed real-fire windows:

1. **Visible realized corruption-family non-nominal behavior**  
   The corruption family should show readable nonzero downshift or guarded occupancy on at least some windows where it previously remained mostly nominal.

2. **Corruption-led interpretation preserved**  
   The resulting behavior should still be explainable in terms of corruption-like degradation under continued activity, not merely generic weak support.

3. **Distinction from opportunistic preserved**  
   The corruption family should not read like a slower opportunistic clone.

4. **Boundedness**  
   The corruption family should not collapse into near-constant downshift or immediate certification.

5. **Auditability**  
   The designer and visualizer should still make it possible to explain why corruption-led posture was entered and why that behavior belongs to this family.

6. **Semantic discipline**  
   Advisory vs active and usefulness vs regime distinctions must remain intact.

These criteria matter more here than raw headline metric improvement.

## 9. Disciplined implementation plan

### 9.1 Step A: retain the existing audit surfaces

Keep the Subgoal 01 and 02 diagnostic surfaces, especially:

- weak-support diagnostics,
- corruption-facing trigger components,
- active realized-state summaries,
- and mechanism-audit traces where relevant.

These remain necessary for proving that corruption-family movement is both visible and interpretable.

### 9.2 Step B: define one explicit corruption-family guarded posture revision

Introduce one bounded corruption-family structure revision, likely involving:

- a corruption-sensitive guarded rung or guarded active interpretation,
- modest but visible posture differentiation from nominal,
- and enough persistence structure that recurrent corruption-like evidence can accumulate into realized movement.

This should remain narrower and less mobility-shaped than opportunistic.

### 9.3 Step C: revise corruption entry semantics carefully

If needed, relax the corruption-family entry semantics **structurally rather than carelessly**.

Possible directions include:

- bounded persistence on corruption-like evidence,
- mild accumulation over repeated corruption-like signal,
- or family-specific guarded entry that does not require the strongest corruption conjunction on a single step.

The goal is not to make corruption easy to fire. The goal is to make it legible on realistic bounded windows.

### 9.4 Step D: keep schema and UI exposure compact

The corruption-family revision should appear through bounded preset/family exposure, not through a broad new editable surface.

The frontend should support disciplined inspection and comparison, not free-form family programming.

### 9.5 Step E: evaluate against the current reference set

Primary evaluation should compare:

- revised corruption family,
- current balanced family,
- current opportunistic family,
- advisory vs active views,
- and the same bounded transformed real-fire windows used in Subgoals 01 and 02.

Synthetic checks remain secondary sanity checks.

### 9.6 Step F: freeze on corruption-family readability

If the corruption family becomes visibly active and remains corruption-led in interpretation, that is enough for Subgoal 03.

This subgoal does not need to prove that corruption becomes the best active family. It only needs to show that the family can exist behaviorally and scientifically as its own thing.

If the first guarded corruption path remains behaviorally inert on bounded real-fire windows, Subgoal 03 may introduce one compact corruption-specific intermediate posture or accumulation seam, provided it remains narrower and less mobility-shaped than the opportunistic family.

## 11. Subgoal 03 scope boundary

This subgoal is intentionally limited.

It is **not** a general redesign of active regime management for real-fire data. It is the next bounded step after Subgoal 02, motivated by the finding that the corruption-focused family still lacks sufficiently readable realized expression on bounded real-fire windows.

The key test is not whether corruption becomes the dominant family. The key test is whether the corruption-focused family can become visibly active, scientifically interpretable, and behaviorally distinct without being flattened into an opportunistic copy.

That is the right next controller-facing objective for AWSRT v0.4 after the Subgoal 02 freeze.

---

## 12. Summary and Results
Subgoal 03 established that the corruption-focused family can be made behaviorally expressible on bounded real-fire windows through a guarded corruption-led downshift path. The resulting expression remained distinct from pure opportunistic weak-support triggering and avoided the earlier dead-family reading. However, the realized behavior still tended to remain downshift-dominant once entered, indicating that corruption-family entry legibility was improved more than corruption-family residence/exit shaping. That follow-on issue should be treated as a separate next-step question rather than folded into further Subgoal 03 threshold chasing.
