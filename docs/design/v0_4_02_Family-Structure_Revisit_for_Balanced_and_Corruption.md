# AWSRT v0.4 Subgoal 02: Family-Structure Revisit for Balanced and Corruption Active Expression

**Status:** Draft design note  
**Applies to:** `v0.4-subgoal-02`  
**Purpose:** Define the next bounded controller-facing step after the Subgoal 01 freeze by revisiting family structure for the balanced and corruption-focused active regime families so they can express readable non-nominal behavior on bounded real-fire windows without collapsing into the opportunistic family.

---

## 1. Purpose of this note

This note defines the second disciplined subgoal of AWSRT v0.4.

Subgoal 01 should now be read as a useful partial success. It confirmed that bounded transformed real-fire windows are a valid controller-facing evaluation surface and that visibly non-nominal active regime behavior can, in fact, be recovered on that surface. However, it also showed that this recovery was not uniform across families.

The main result of Subgoal 01 was not simply that one more trigger adjustment helped or failed. The deeper result was that:

- the **opportunistic** family could already express visible active downshift,
- the **balanced** family showed only weak realized non-nominal behavior,
- and the **corruption-focused** family remained mostly nominal despite bounded trigger-side recalibration.

That changes the next question.

The immediate problem is no longer primarily whether another local trigger tweak can rescue balanced and corruption behavior. The immediate problem is whether the **family structures themselves** are still too shallow, too compressed, or too weakly differentiated to express readable active posture changes on bounded real-fire windows.

Subgoal 02 therefore asks:

> What is the smallest bounded family-structure revision that lets the balanced and corruption-focused families express non-nominal active behavior on bounded real-fire windows without flattening them into opportunistic copies?

The stance remains disciplined. This is not a license for broad redesign. It is a bounded structural revisit motivated by evidence produced in Subgoal 01.

---

## 2. Background and framing

### 2.1 v0.1 scientific story

AWSRT v0.1 established the platform’s core scientific story:

- time-to-first-detection is necessary but insufficient,
- informational activity and belief-improving value can diverge,
- and different impairment types produce meaningfully different operational consequences.

That story remains the right scientific background for controller work. An active regime family should not be judged mainly by whether it moves often, nor by whether it optimizes a single metric. It should be judged by whether its posture changes are operationally intelligible under the kinds of support, uncertainty, and degradation patterns the platform can reveal.

### 2.2 v0.2 interpretive cleanup

AWSRT v0.2 clarified the main semantic boundaries that still govern controller-facing work:

- usefulness remained distinguishable from higher-level regime posture,
- advisory and active semantics were separated more honestly,
- active mechanism behavior became inspectable rather than merely implied,
- and the platform became more disciplined about what was live versus what remained surface exposure.

These constraints remain binding in v0.4. A family-structure revisit must not blur the layers simply because the controller is hard to animate. Any structural change must still preserve:

- usefulness as lower-level evidence interpretation,
- regime as higher-level posture,
- advisory as recommendation-side output,
- and active as realized controller behavior.

### 2.3 v0.3 bounded real-fire outcome

AWSRT v0.3 established that bounded transformed real-fire windows are already scientifically and operationally interpretable enough to serve as real evaluation targets. It also showed that bounded corruption-side patching was not enough to recover the hoped-for non-nominal active reading.

This remains the bridge into v0.4: the problem is now grounded in bounded real-fire behavior, not only in synthetic or semi-synthetic controller diagnostics.

### 2.4 v0.4 Subgoal 01 result

Subgoal 01 added a bounded weak-support active downshift path and improved diagnostic visibility. That pass produced a clear success in the opportunistic family, weak movement in the balanced family, and little change in the corruption-focused family.

This should be interpreted carefully.

The important lesson is not merely that one family “worked better.” The important lesson is that the opportunistic family had enough internal posture structure to make active regime movement visible, while the other two families likely did not. That suggests the remaining bottleneck is not just trigger sensitivity. It is at least partly **family expressiveness**.

## 3. Control-language discipline for this subgoal

The control-language glossary remains active guidance here.

For Subgoal 02:

- **usefulness** remains lower-level evidence about the operational value of current information flow;
- **regime** remains a higher-level posture decision;
- **advisory** remains recommendation-side interpretation;
- **active** remains realized behavior;
- **family** refers to a bounded regime-management configuration with its own posture shape and intended reading, not merely a collection of thresholds.

This subgoal does **not** authorize collapsing balanced, corruption, and opportunistic into one generic active controller. In fact, its core purpose is the opposite: to preserve family meanings while giving each family enough structure to express those meanings behaviorally.

This means Subgoal 02 should not be read as:

- “make balanced more like opportunistic,” or
- “make corruption more like opportunistic.”

Instead, it should be read as:

- identify what structural ingredients made opportunistic readable,
- extract only the minimum necessary structural lessons,
- and reapply those lessons in family-specific ways.

That distinction matters because otherwise the platform could drift into one active family with cosmetic labels, which would be scientifically weaker than staying partially nominal but honest.

---

## 4. Problem statement

The present problem can be stated more precisely as follows:

> On bounded transformed real-fire windows, balanced and corruption-focused active regime families still do not express readable non-nominal realized behavior, even after bounded trigger-side recalibration. We need a bounded structural revision that gives those families enough posture shape to become behaviorally legible without erasing their distinct meanings.

The key issue is no longer whether a trigger can be made to fire occasionally. The issue is whether the family has a meaningful behavioral space in which a trigger can produce an interpretable realized posture.

This means Subgoal 02 should be judged less by whether any nonzero transition occurs, and more by whether family behavior becomes:

- visibly realized,
- structurally legible,
- semantically family-specific,
- and experimentally readable on bounded real-fire windows.

---

## 5. Why structure now appears to be the main bottleneck

The most plausible explanations are the following.

### 5.1 Balanced may still be too shallow to express downshift

In Subgoal 01, balanced produced only weak realized active downshift even after weak-support sensitivity was added. That suggests the issue is not only that balanced lacked a trigger path. It suggests that balanced may still lack enough intermediate posture structure to occupy a readable downshift state before returning nominally or collapsing into certified-like behavior.

A family with too little internal structure may technically transition while still looking behaviorally nominal.

### 5.2 Corruption-focused active behavior may be too compressed

The corruption-focused family appears to need more than looser corruption-side thresholds. If the family has no meaningful guarded posture between nominal and stronger interventions, then corruption-sensitive signals may still fail to produce interpretable realized behavior.

This suggests the corruption family may need a structurally clearer active reading, not merely a stronger corruption trigger.

### 5.3 Opportunistic succeeded because it had posture shape, not only trigger sensitivity

The opportunistic family should now be treated as evidence that visible active regime behavior on bounded real-fire windows requires internal posture shape:

- multiple ladder levels,
- enough motion/behavioral spacing between levels,
- enough time and room for downshift occupancy to appear,
- and a certified boundary that does not immediately swallow the story.

This does **not** mean opportunistic is “the correct controller.” It means it is the current best proof that family structure can matter more than one more threshold tweak.

### 5.4 Family identity is now the main scientific risk

Once structure becomes the main lever, the danger shifts. In Subgoal 01 the main risk was over-tuning triggers. In Subgoal 02 the main risk is erasing family meanings by making balanced and corruption mere variants of opportunistic.

That means Subgoal 02 must remain comparative and interpretive, not just behavior-maximizing.

### 5.5 Readability now depends on structural legibility

Subgoal 01 showed that auditability alone is not enough. We can now inspect more signals than before, but balanced and corruption still do not move much. The next requirement is that the **family itself** becomes readable.

This means a family should be structured so that when it does move, we can say:

- what posture it left,
- what intermediate posture it entered,
- and why that movement belongs to that family rather than another one.

---

## 6. Subgoal decision: bounded structural revisit rather than more micro-patching

Subgoal 02 should **not** continue the Subgoal 01 pattern of trying one more micro-patch on the same shallow family surfaces.

The recommended direction is:

- **not** a redesign of the whole regime-management layer,
- **not** a large free-form parameterization of family shapes,
- **not** copying the opportunistic ladder into all families,
- but **yes** to a bounded structural revisit of the balanced and corruption-focused active families.

The reason is now evidence-based. Subgoal 01 showed that trigger-side adjustments alone are not the main bottleneck. The remaining difficulty is whether the intended families have enough behavioral structure to express their meanings on bounded real-fire windows.

A bounded structural revisit is therefore the smaller scientifically honest move.

---

## 7. Proposed v0.4 Subgoal 02 direction

### 7.1 Core recommendation

Subgoal 02 should introduce one bounded family-structure revision for **balanced** and one bounded family-structure revision for **corruption-focused** active behavior.

The intended shift is:

- from asking whether the existing shallow families can be animated by better trigger seams,
- toward asking whether each family needs a slightly richer posture shape to become behaviorally legible.

### 7.2 Balanced family direction

Balanced should likely receive a **minimal multi-rung active structure**.

The goal is not to make balanced highly expressive or aggressively opportunistic. The goal is to allow balanced to express a modest, bounded, intermediate active downshift posture that is still recognizably balanced.

The desired reading is:

- nominal balanced posture,
- tempered or guarded balanced posture,
- then stronger intervention only if needed.

Balanced should still read as moderate and measured, not as a high-mobility opportunistic family.

### 7.3 Corruption-focused family direction

The corruption-focused family should likely receive a **corruption-sensitive guarded structure**.

Its problem appears different from balanced. Balanced seems structurally too shallow. Corruption seems both structurally compressed and trigger-side underexpressive. So the corruption family likely needs:

- a distinct guarded corruption-sensitive posture,
- continued emphasis on corruption-like concern,
- and enough structural space that corruption-sensitive conditions can produce visible realized behavior before the family collapses back to nominal or skips to stronger intervention.

The corruption family should still read as corruption-led, not as generic support-collapse behavior.

### 7.4 Opportunistic family role in Subgoal 02

The opportunistic family should be retained mainly as a **reference success family**.

It is not the direct template for the others. It is the comparison case that tells us:

- readable active downshift is possible,
- bounded real-fire windows can support it,
- and structural posture differentiation matters.

Subgoal 02 should therefore compare against opportunistic, not clone it.

### 7.5 What should not change

Subgoal 02 should not:

- unify all family structures into a common ladder,
- redesign the full regime-management surface,
- merge advisory and active semantics,
- claim optimized real-fire control,
- or explode the family design into a broad configurable surface.

The changes should remain compact, auditable, and family-specific.

---

## 8. Success criteria

Subgoal 02 should be considered successful if it produces all of the following on bounded transformed real-fire windows:

1. **Readable non-nominal active behavior in balanced**  
   Balanced should show more than token downshift occupancy and should do so in a way that still reads as moderate, not opportunistic.

2. **Readable non-nominal active behavior in corruption-focused runs**  
   Corruption-focused active behavior should become visibly realized without losing its corruption-sensitive interpretation.

3. **Family distinction preserved**  
   Balanced, corruption, and opportunistic should remain behaviorally distinguishable.

4. **No semantic collapse**  
   Advisory and active outputs must remain distinct, and regime posture must not collapse into usefulness-state replay.

5. **Boundedness**  
   The new structures should not make balanced or corruption nearly always downshifted or nearly always certified.

6. **Auditability**  
   The visualizer and summaries must still support clear explanation of why a posture was entered and why it belongs to that family.

These criteria matter more here than broad headline metric gains.

## 9. Disciplined implementation plan

### 9.1 Step A: preserve the Subgoal 01 audit surfaces

Retain the weak-support, corruption, and realized active-state diagnostics introduced or clarified in Subgoal 01.

They are now part of the controller-facing audit surface and should continue to support interpretation in Subgoal 02.

### 9.2 Step B: define one minimal balanced-family structure revision

Introduce one explicit balanced-family active structure revision, likely involving:

- a small number of ladder levels,
- modest motion differentiation,
- and a clearly bounded guarded posture.

This should remain visibly milder than opportunistic.

### 9.3 Step C: define one minimal corruption-family structure revision

Introduce one explicit corruption-sensitive structure revision, likely involving:

- a guarded corruption-sensitive rung or posture,
- modest structural space between nominal and stronger intervention,
- and retained corruption-facing entry semantics.

This should remain visibly corruption-led, not merely support-led.

### 9.4 Step D: keep schema and UI exposure compact

Expose the new family structures through bounded presets or compact family selectors, not through a wide free-form structural programming surface.

The frontend should support disciplined experimentation, not arbitrary family authoring.

### 9.5 Step E: compare against the Subgoal 01 reference set

The main evaluation set should include:

- revised balanced family,
- revised corruption-focused family,
- opportunistic reference family,
- advisory/active comparison,
- and the same bounded transformed real-fire windows used in Subgoal 01.

Synthetic cross-checks should remain secondary.

### 9.6 Step F: freeze on family readability, not overreach

If the revised balanced and corruption-focused families become visibly active and interpretable without collapsing into opportunistic clones, that is enough for Subgoal 02.

This subgoal does not need to prove controller superiority. It needs to show that family structure can be revised in a bounded, honest, scientifically readable way.

## 11. Subgoal 02 scope boundary

This subgoal is intentionally limited.

It is **not** a full redesign of active regime management for real-fire data. It is the next bounded step after Subgoal 01, motivated by the finding that trigger-side recalibration alone was not enough to recover readable active behavior in all intended families.

The key test is not whether every family becomes equally expressive. The key test is whether the balanced and corruption-focused families can be given enough structure to become visibly active and scientifically interpretable while preserving their distinct meanings.

That is the right next controller-facing objective for AWSRT v0.4 after the Subgoal 01 freeze.
