# AWSRT v0.2 Subgoal M: Curated Results Packaging

**Status:** Draft design note  
**Applies to:** `v0.2-subgoal-m`  
**Purpose:** Define the penultimate v0.2 step after Subgoal L by producing a compact, disciplined, release-facing results bundle that captures what v0.2 now adds scientifically and operationally, without reopening controller design or expanding into a new large experimentation campaign.

---

## 1. Scope

This note defines **Subgoal M** for AWSRT v0.2.

Subgoal L completed the validation and audit hardening step. It strengthened confidence that:

- compact usefulness behaves coherently,
- advisory and active regime-management outputs are reported more truthfully,
- mechanism-audit availability is more honest,
- and the current v0.2 operational layer is substantially easier to trust.

That means the next disciplined move should not yet be final freeze packaging.

Before final freeze, v0.2 should leave behind a **curated results bundle** that shows, in a compact and interpretable way, what this version of the platform now supports.

Subgoal M is therefore a **curated results packaging** subgoal.

It is not:

- a new controller-design subgoal,
- a broad experimentation subgoal,
- a thesis-chapter-scale benchmarking pass,
- or a broad UI redesign subgoal.

---

## 2. Why Subgoal M is the right next step

L made the current operational layer more trustworthy, but a frozen release should not rest only on cleanup and validation. It should also leave behind a compact set of representative studies and runs that make the v0.2 contribution visible.

By the end of L, v0.2 has a distinct enough story to justify that concluding evidence bundle:

- compact usefulness is a real, behaviorally active path,
- regime-management remains a broader advisory/active layer,
- advisory and active semantics are more clearly separated,
- and audit/mechanism surfaces are more truthful and more legible.

M should therefore convert that progress into a small, stable, interpretable result set that can support release notes, thesis framing, and later publication-oriented writing.

M should remain much smaller than the v0.1 results campaign. It does not need to recreate the full v0.1 comparison chapter. It only needs to capture what v0.2 adds:

- usefulness-aware operational interpretation,
- clearer advisory vs active semantics,
- and more mechanistically legible regime-managed behavior.

The final v0.2 freeze should rest on:

- boundary clarity from J,
- structural cleanup from K,
- validation/audit hardening from L,
- and a compact release-facing evidence bundle from M.

---

## 3. Main development question

The central question for Subgoal M is:

> what is the smallest curated set of runs, studies, and supporting surface refinements needed to make AWSRT v0.2 legible as a completed, evidence-backed checkpoint rather than only a validated code state?

Equivalently:

> what compact result bundle best shows what v0.2 now adds, without reopening controller design or launching a full new results campaign?

M is a **results-packaging** question, not a **new scientific discovery** subgoal.

---

## 4. Development stance

Subgoal M should preserve the late-stage v0.2 discipline:

- preserve the L checkpoint,
- preserve the J boundary interpretation,
- avoid broad controller redesign,
- avoid large route/schema churn,
- avoid broad frontend redesign,
- prefer screenshot-friendly, interpretable outputs,
- and produce a compact release/thesis-usable bundle rather than an exhaustive benchmark suite.

M is:

- **curation-oriented**,
- **interpretation-oriented**,
- **release-supporting**,
- and **modest in scope**.

It should not become:

- a new exploratory policy campaign,
- a re-litigation of controller semantics,
- a full v0.1-style thesis chapter rewrite,
- or a major analysis-platform rebuild.

---

## 5. Working diagnosis entering Subgoal M

The main remaining gap after L is **evidence packaging**, not **code trustworthiness**.

At the end of v0.2, it should be possible to point to a small set of outputs and say:

- this bundle shows compact usefulness behaving interpretably,
- this bundle shows advisory and active regime semantics cleanly separated,
- and this bundle shows regime-managed behavior can now be read more mechanistically rather than only impressionistically.

Some analysis-surface refinement may be warranted, but only if it materially improves clarity or screenshot-readiness. M should remain controlled. Its purpose is not to discover all possible v0.2 effects. Its purpose is to leave behind a disciplined final set of representative outputs.

---

## 6. Main v0.2 story M should support

Subgoal M should package v0.2 around the following compact interpretive story.

### 6.1 Compact usefulness is now operationally interpretable
A curated v0.2 bundle should show that compact usefulness:

- stays in exploit under healthy conditions,
- tends toward recover under delay/staleness-dominated degradation,
- tends toward caution under corruption/noise-like degradation,
- and does so with supporting summaries that are now easier to trust.

### 6.2 Advisory and active regime semantics are now legible
A curated v0.2 bundle should show that:

- advisory outputs are recommendation/trigger-hit surfaces,
- active outputs are realized-state/realized-transition surfaces,
- and the two are no longer casually conflated.

### 6.3 Regime-managed behavior is now more inspectable
A curated v0.2 bundle should also show that one can read active regime behavior through:

- realized state occupancy,
- realized transitions,
- effective controls,
- and mechanism-audit support traces,

without claiming that v0.2 has solved final controller optimization.

### 6.4 v0.2 adds disciplined operational legibility more than broad new science
The main value of M is not to claim a completely new scientific chapter on top of v0.1. It is to show that the wedge-informed operational/control direction has become more structured, more legible, and more trustworthy in v0.2.

## 7. Curated study bundle

The following compact bundle is now the right M target.

### 7.1 Bundle A — compact usefulness triad
A minimal triad of representative single-run outputs:

- one ideal / near-ideal `usefulness_proto` run,
- one delay-heavy `usefulness_proto` run,
- one noise-heavy `usefulness_proto` run.

Purpose:

- show exploit / recover / caution interpretations cleanly,
- show the delay-vs-noise distinction operationally,
- and support the claim that compact usefulness is now readable.

Current interpretation:

- the healthy case is exploit-dominated,
- the delay-heavy case gives the clearest recover-style reading,
- and the noise-heavy case gives the clearest caution-style reading.

### 7.2 Bundle B — advisory vs active regime comparison
A small study bundle comparing:

- one advisory family study,
- one active family study.

Purpose:

- show that advisory and active outputs support different interpretations,
- and show that the analysis surfaces now present those differences more clearly.

This should be read primarily as a **study-level semantic comparison bundle**, not as the deep mechanism bundle.

### 7.3 Bundle C — opportunistic-family mechanism package
One active opportunistic-family mechanism package, now best understood as three linked artifacts:

- **C1** — representative active opportunistic run,
- **C2** — opportunistic-family hysteresis sensitivity study,
- **C2V** — verify-style opportunistic mechanism-audit run.

Purpose:

- show that opportunistic active behavior is now readable,
- show that hysteresis sensitivity is broad-plateau rather than knife-edge,
- and show that verify-style traces can expose recovery / leave-certified / transition mechanics when desired.

Recommended reading:

- **C1** is the representative default active opportunistic run,
- **C2** is the family-level mechanism-sensitivity study,
- **C2V** is the verify-style mechanism-audit example.

### 7.4 Optional supporting family
A smaller balanced or certified supporting bundle may still be acceptable if it materially improves interpretive balance, but it should remain optional rather than mandatory.

The current curated bundle is already sufficient without forcing an additional family campaign.

## 8. Candidate M outputs

Subgoal M should leave behind the following compact outputs.

### 8.1 Compact usefulness screenshot set
This should summarize:

- usefulness state behavior,
- trigger activity,
- rolling support quantities,
- and the delay-vs-noise distinction.

### 8.2 Advisory vs active comparison figure
This should summarize:

- advisory trigger-hit summaries,
- active realized occupancy summaries,
- transition counts,
- and effective eta / motion summaries.

### 8.3 Mechanism-sensitivity figure
This is naturally represented by the **opportunistic hysteresis study**.

Its purpose is to show:

- how realized active transitions change across hysteresis settings,
- that the useful operating region is a broad middle plateau,
- and that very large hysteresis suppresses realized transitions back toward base-like behavior.

### 8.4 Verify-style mechanism-audit screenshot set
This should come from the opportunistic verify run and should be explicitly diagnostic.

Its purpose is to show:

- realized state trace,
- recovery/caution persistence behavior,
- leave-certified evidence,
- and effective-control traces.

### 8.5 Compact release/thesis reference table
A small table of the curated runs/studies is useful for later writing and screenshot reuse.

## 10. What M should not change

Subgoal M should avoid changing all of the following unless a very small clarity fix is unavoidable:

- compact usefulness controller identity,
- usefulness thresholds,
- advisory regime semantics,
- active regime semantics,
- endpoint routes broadly,
- operational series names broadly,
- or summary schema names broadly.

M should also avoid:

- launching a fresh large impairment campaign,
- trying to re-establish the entire v0.1 wedge chapter from scratch,
- broad UI/platform redesign,
- or post-v0.2 architecture cleanup under the label of results packaging.

---

## 11. Success criteria

Subgoal M should be considered successful if:

- v0.2 ends with a compact, interpretable results bundle,
- compact usefulness behavior is clearly illustrated,
- advisory and active regime semantics are clearly distinguished in the final outputs,
- at least one active mechanism-sensitivity study is screenshot/readout ready,
- at least one verify-style mechanism-audit run is screenshot/readout ready,
- only modest analysis-surface changes were required,
- and the resulting outputs are strong enough to support release/thesis-facing interpretation.

A strong success outcome would be:

- v0.2 can now be described as structurally cleaner, validation-hardened, and accompanied by a small curated evidence bundle showing what it adds.

---

## 12. Warning signs

M should be treated as drifting or unsuccessful if:

- it turns into a large new results campaign,
- it tries to revisit all of v0.1’s experimental questions,
- it broadens into large frontend work,
- it introduces major new summary/schema complexity,
- or it becomes unclear which outputs are actually part of the final curated bundle.

Another warning sign would be producing many studies but no clear release-facing set of outputs.

M should prioritize a small number of clean, interpretable deliverables.

---

## 13. Relationship to the final v0.2 step

### 13.1 Subgoal N — final freeze and release packaging
If M succeeds, the final v0.2 step can focus on:

- final freeze interpretation,
- release note / design note packaging,
- final tag/release discipline,
- and a concise statement of what v0.2 now is and is not.

### 13.2 Post-v0.2 work
M is not the place for broader post-v0.2 ambitions such as:

- larger router decomposition,
- broader analysis-platform redesign,
- deeper controller unification,
- or major new validation infrastructure.

Those remain future work.

---

## 14. Recommended freeze interpretation for M

If successful, Subgoal M should be frozen as:

- a **curated results packaging checkpoint**,
- preserving the J boundary interpretation,
- building on the K cleanup and L validation hardening,
- producing a compact final v0.2 study bundle,
- and leaving the project ready for the final freeze and release packaging step.

More concretely, the M freeze should be able to say:

- Bundle A shows compact usefulness in healthy / delay-heavy / noise-heavy readings,
- Bundle B shows advisory-versus-active study semantics cleanly,
- and Bundle C shows that opportunistic active behavior is now readable both as a family-level sensitivity study and as a verify-style mechanism audit.

---

## 15. Short summary

Subgoal M is the curated results packaging step after Subgoal L. Its purpose is to produce a compact, disciplined, release-facing evidence bundle that shows what AWSRT v0.2 now adds: interpretable compact usefulness behavior, clearer advisory-versus-active regime semantics, and more legible active mechanism behavior. M should remain modest. It is not a new controller-design or broad experimentation step. It should favor a small set of screenshot-ready studies and representative runs, with only modest analysis-surface refinements where necessary. In the current bundle, Bundle A provides the usefulness triad, Bundle B provides the advisory-versus-active study comparison, and Bundle C is best understood as a three-part opportunistic-family mechanism package: **C1** as the representative active opportunistic run, **C2** as the opportunistic hysteresis sensitivity study, and **C2V** as the verify-style mechanism-audit run. M succeeds if it leaves v0.2 with both a validated operational layer and a compact curated results bundle, ready for a final freeze/release packaging step in Subgoal N.
